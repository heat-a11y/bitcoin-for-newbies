🤦 Myths That Need to Die

❌ "Only used by criminals."
Cash is used by criminals too. Blockchains are actually more traceable. Legitimate users, companies, and countries use Bitcoin every day.
❌ "It's a bubble that will go to zero."
People have called it a bubble every year since 2011. It's still here. Volatility is real, but it's become a trillion-dollar asset used by institutions worldwide.
❌ "Bad for the environment."
Over 50% of mining uses renewable energy. Bitcoin incentivizes green energy development (stranded energy, methane capture).
❌ "I need to buy a whole Bitcoin."
Bitcoin is divisible to 8 decimals. You can buy $10 worth. The smallest unit is a satoshi: 0.00000001 BTC.
❌ "It's too late."
Less than 5% of the world owns Bitcoin. Adoption is still early. The best time to learn was years ago. The second best is now.

🎯 How to Start

  1. Download a wallet app — Try Phoenix, Blue Wallet, or Muun.
  2. Buy a small amount — Use Strike, Relai, or Coinbase. Start with $20–$50.
  3. Send a test transaction — Send 0.0001 BTC to a friend. Once you do it, it clicks.
  4. Learn self-custody — Phone wallet for small amounts. Hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) for larger.
  5. Ignore the noise — Don't check price daily. Don't join "pump" groups. Hold what you can afford to lose.

❓ Quick Answers

Is Bitcoin legal?

Yes in most countries. US, EU, UK, Japan, Australia, and most of the world allow it.

Do I need to be a computer genius?

Nope. If you can use a banking app, you can use Bitcoin.

Can I lose my Bitcoin?

Yes — if you lose your private keys. Start small and learn safety first.

What makes it worth anything?

Same as gold: trust + scarcity + utility. Bitcoin is digital gold you can send instantly.
